Grammar Clinic Passive Voice — Step by Step ⏱ 25 min ▼
🎯 The passive voice causes confusion because it looks very different from active sentences. This clinic breaks it down step by step — why we use it, how to build it in each tense, and five different ways to practise it.

The passive voice shifts the focus from who does the action to what happens. Compare these two situations:

Active — we focus on the person

The FTC banned the ad.

Someone criticized the campaign.

A company produces the product.

Passive — we focus on the thing

The ad was banned by the FTC.

The campaign was criticized.

The product is produced by a company.

🔑 Three reasons to use the passive
  1. The action matters more than the person. "The ad was removed." (We care about the ad, not who removed it.)
  2. We don't know who did it. "The window was broken." (Nobody knows who.)
  3. We don't want to say who did it. "A mistake was made." (Very common in formal/official language!)

👉 Click How to Build It to see the five tense formulas.

The passive is always built the same way: the correct form of "be" + the past participle. The tense changes the form of "be" — the past participle never changes.

Simple Present
is / are + past participle
Ads are controlled by the FTC.
Present Progressive
is / are + being + past participle
The ad is being reviewed right now.
Simple Past
was / were + past participle
The campaign was banned last year.
Present Perfect
has / have + been + past participle
The ad has been removed already.
Past Perfect
had + been + past participle
It had been seen by millions before the ban.
⚠️ Common Mistakes
❌ The ad was remove. → ✅ was removed
❌ is being control. → ✅ is being controlled
❌ have been saw. → ✅ have been seen

Speaking 🎙 Ad Talk — Two Activities ⏱ 22 min ▼
🎯 Two back-to-back speaking activities. The first is a fast pairs game using passive voice to describe an ad. The second is a richer group discussion using conversation cards.

🎮 Activity 1 — Guess the Ad (10 min, pairs)

Think of a real ad — from TV, social media, or a billboard. Describe it to your partner using passive voice without saying the brand name. Your partner guesses. Then swap.

Use at least two different passive tenses in your description.

🃏 Activity 2 — Ad Conversation Cards (10 min, groups of 4–5)

Click a card to reveal a discussion question. Everyone must answer before you move to the next card. Use passive structures and today's vocabulary in your answers whenever possible.

Card 1
Has an ad ever been banned in your country that you think should NOT have been? What happened?
💡 Use: was banned, was criticized, was removed, had been seen
Card 2
Do you think advertising directed at children should be controlled more strictly? Why?
💡 Use: is controlled, should be banned, are being reviewed
Card 3
Think about an ad you have been influenced by. Why was it so effective? How was it made?
💡 Use: was made by, was shown on, was targeted at, has been seen
Card 4
Should celebrities be held responsible for the products they are paid to promote? Why?
💡 Use: are paid to, is promoted by, was criticized for, has been used
Card 5
Have any ads been shown recently that you found offensive or inappropriate? What should be done?
💡 Use: should be removed, has been criticized, was complained about
Card 6
Do you think advertising works? Has your opinion on this been changed by anything you've seen today?
💡 Use: has been changed, was influenced, has been shown, is known for
💡 Keep Talking
After each answer, react to what your classmate said: "Can we hear someone else's opinion?" / "What do you think, [name]?" — Don't move to the next card until everyone has spoken.
